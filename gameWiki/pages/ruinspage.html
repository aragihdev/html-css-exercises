The Ruins

It's the location where Frisk first falls into the Underground and where the player is introduced to the fundamental mechanics of combat, interaction, and puzzle-solving.

Originally, the Ruins served as the capital of the Underground, known as Home. Following the war between humans and monsters, the monster kind settled here before expanding into the rest of the realm. Over time, the population migrated to "New Home," leaving the Ruins in a state of neglect and silence. Currently, the area serves as an isolated sanctuary maintained by Toriel, who seeks to protect any human who falls there from the intentions of King Asgore.

Geographical Characteristics

The aesthetic of the Ruins is defined by shades of purple and magenta, featuring stone structures that show clear signs of age. Vegetation is sparse, consisting primarily of piles of red leaves and the iconic patches of Golden Flowers that cushion the fall of humans.

Key Locations

  • The Flowerbed: The starting point where the game begins.
  • The Room of Flow: Where the first encounter with Flowey takes place under a stone archway.
  • The Long Hallway: An extensive area where Toriel tests the protagonist's independence and patience, asking them to wait for her.
  • The Candy Pedestal: A spot where the player can take "Monster Candy," though the game advises against being greedy.
  • The Spider Bake Sale: Small webs where gold can be deposited to receive items made by spiders.
  • Toriel's Home: An impeccably clean and cozy residence at the end of the region. It features a bedroom for the protagonist and a kitchen that smells of cinnamon and butterscotch pie.

Inhabitants and Monsters

The monster fauna in the Ruins is generally less aggressive than in other areas, serving as a training ground for the player.

NPCs: In addition to Toriel, players encounter the ghost Napstablook, who pretends to be asleep to avoid interaction, and several Froggits who offer advice regarding the game's interface.

Random Encounters: These include Whimsun (who flees easily), Loox (who dislikes being stared at), Vegetoid (who encourages healthy eating), Migosp, and Parsnik.

Gameplay Mechanics

In this area, the player learns about Save Points and the manifestation of Determination. The puzzles focus on:

  • Pressing switches in the correct order.
  • Navigating rooms with floors that give way if the wrong path is taken.
  • Moving through rooms with mirror-like layouts or color patterns.

Impact on Routes

The player's actions in the Ruins set the tone for the rest of the experience:

Pacifist/Neutral Route: Sparing the monsters ( and especially Toriel ) results in an emotional farewell where she allows the player to leave but locks the door behind them for their own safety.

Genocide Route: If the player defeats every monster until the phrase "But nobody came" appears, the atmosphere shifts drastically. The music slows down, NPCs disappear, and Toriel realizes she isn't protecting the human from the monsters, but rather protecting the monsters from the human.
